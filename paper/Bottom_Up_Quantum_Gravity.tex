\begin{abstract}

We propose a minimal framework in which space, time and gravity emerge from the entanglement structure of a finite global quantum state without any prior spacetime background.

Geometry is reconstructed from mutual information between subsystems and embedded using multidimensional scaling. The effective spatial dimension depends on the entanglement structure and transitions between different regimes depending on the locality of correlations.

Several operational signatures are demonstrated numerically on systems of $N=9$ and $N=16$ qubits:

\begin{itemize}
\item relational time via modular flow,
\item emergent geometry via information distance reconstruction,
\item thermodynamic gravity via a Jacobson-like entropy-energy relation,
\item intrinsic geometry via spectral dimension,
\item wormhole-like ER=EPR signatures,
\item chaotic modular dynamics characterized by Random Matrix Theory,
\item horizon-like information bottlenecks in entanglement graphs.
\end{itemize}

We further identify a topology-dependent modular constant
\[
C = d_A g_2^{\mathrm{plateau}}
\]
revealing that the modular Hamiltonian spectrum retains geometric information about the underlying entanglement structure.

\end{abstract}

\section{Emergent Time via Modular Flow}

For a subsystem $A$

\[
\rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_{\bar A} |\Psi\rangle\langle\Psi|
\]

the modular Hamiltonian is

\[
K_A = -\log\rho_A
\]

which generates modular evolution

\[
O(\tau)=e^{iK_A\tau}Oe^{-iK_A\tau}.
\]

This defines relational time analogous to the Page–Wootters mechanism \cite{pagewootters}.

\section{Modular Chaos}

\subsection{Gap statistics}

Eigenvalues of $K_A$ are analyzed through the gap ratio

\[
r_n=\frac{\min(\Delta_n,\Delta_{n+1})}{\max(\Delta_n,\Delta_{n+1})}.
\]

\section{Emergent Geometry}

Mutual information

\[
I(i:j)=S(\rho_i)+S(\rho_j)-S(\rho_{ij})
\]

defines the information distance

\[
d_{ij}=-\log\frac{I(i:j)}{I_{\max}+\epsilon}.
\]

Points are embedded using multidimensional scaling to reconstruct geometry \cite{vanraamsdonk}.

\section{Spectral Geometry}

We construct a weighted graph with

\[
w_{ij}=I(i:j).
\]

The Laplacian

\[
L=D-W
\]

defines the heat trace

\[
Z(\tau)=Tr(e^{-\tau L}).
\]

The spectral dimension

\[
d_s(\tau)=-2\frac{d\log Z}{d\log\tau}
\]

yields

\[
d_{IR}\approx2.5-2.7.
\]

\section{Thermodynamic Gravity}

We test a Jacobson-like relation \cite{jacobson}

\[
\delta S\approx \beta_{eff}\delta E.
\]

Numerical experiments confirm approximate validity across $\lambda$ variations.

\section{Discrete Curvature and Einstein-like Response}

Cut entropy

\[
S_{cut}(A)=\sum_{i\in A}\sum_{j\notin A}w_{ij}
\]

is related to Ollivier–Ricci curvature measured on the entanglement graph.

Perturbations satisfy an operational relation

\[
\Delta S_{cut}\approx a\Delta R+b\Delta M.
\]

\begin{itemize}
\item $S(A)$ is the region entropy

\section{Conclusion}

Bottom-Up Quantum Gravity proposes that spacetime geometry may emerge from the entanglement structure of quantum states without any prior geometric background.

Even small quantum systems ($N=9,16$) display multiple consistent signatures:

\begin{itemize}
\item emergent dimension tunable by entanglement locality, transitioning from approximately 2D for local entanglement to higher-dimensional effective geometries when non-local correlations dominate
\item chaotic modular dynamics with topology-dependent constant $C$, revealing that the modular Hamiltonian spectrum retains geometric information
\item operational ER=EPR shortcuts where topologically distant qubits become geometrically close in the emergent metric
\item horizon-like information bottlenecks detectable algorithmically, with the region $\{0,2,3,5,6,10,11,15\}$ exhibiting extremely low conductance (2.7th percentile) and exceptionally high internal entanglement (98.8th percentile)
\item triangular structure of multipartite correlations encoded by pairwise mutual informations (AUC $\approx 0.73$, $p<5\times10^{-3}$)
\item approximate thermodynamic gravity via a Jacobson-like entropy-energy relation
\item discrete Einstein-like response relation under perturbations
\end{itemize}

These results suggest that spacetime may be an effective description of the information structure of quantum states, and that key gravitational phenomena — including horizons — admit a purely information-theoretic origin accessible at finite $N$. The identification of a topology-dependent modular constant $C$ provides a concrete bridge between entanglement structure and emergent geometry, while the bottom-up horizon detection algorithm offers an operational tool for identifying black-hole-like regions without any prior geometric input.
